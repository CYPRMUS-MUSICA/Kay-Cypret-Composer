Bio

Kay Cypret (b. 1990)

Kay Cypret is a modern classical composer. Kay earned her D.A. in Music Composition at the University of Northern Colorado, received her M.M. degree in Music Composition and Orchestral Conducting from the University of Missouri-Columbia, and was awarded her BA from Missouri Southern State University. She has composed numerous pieces for chamber and large ensembles.

Kay has written works for the Atlantic Music Festival, NorthStar Duo, Bearthoven, Missouri New Music Initiative, Stacey Barelos, Golden Spectra, Flutist Camden Sidenstricker, Clarinetist Dr. Cheryl Cifelli-Cotter, 1000 Words; Music and Poetry inspired by Photo Spiva, the University of Missouri Tuba-Euphonium Ensemble, and various orchestras.

As a composer, her primary goals are to create a diverse composition portfolio that pushes the boundaries of music, produce scholarly research that challenges current trends, and enrich humanity. She prefers to explore tonal, atonal, and modern syntax in acoustic and electroacoustic mediums.

Kay believes that music is not only an art but one of the highest forms of communication; moreover, music can test our minds, capture our hearts, enrapture our spirits, and bring joy to our lives.
